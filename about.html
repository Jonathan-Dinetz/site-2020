ABOUT
My name is Jonathan Dinetz. I study graphic design at The Rhode Island School of Design, but am taking the year off as a result of COVID-19. I have previously studied at UCLA and Brown, taking classes in Philosophy, Computer Science, and Global Studies among others.

I have co-founded a non-profit organization, lead design for an app aimed at improving health care in Niger, and completed several small freelance projects.

Looking forward, I intend to develop a better understanding of histories—not just of communication—but of a wider range of topics such as psychology, behavioral science, and anthropology. I believe all these subjects should play a central role in a designers problem solving process, informing more traditional design decisions of type and color while also driving the development of more unique and radical solutions when appropriate. Ultimately, I hope to further develop as an organic problem solver. I believe it is counter productive and boring to assume a mode of solution before considering the problem in detail (i.e. “Well it’s a campaign and all campaigns seem to have posters, so lets do that!”). Instead, I aim to let the solution grow naturally from the problem, never presuming that an app, brand identity, or publication must look one way or another unless reasoning supports it. What if the solution is a one act play performed in the middle of Times Square? What if we made the campaign’s posters out of orange peels? I have yet to reach such a level of freedom, but it is what I am striving for.
