Challenge

Hope & Anchor Hardware is a fictional hardware store I was assigned to develop branding for during Color Studio at RISD. Being that the hardware store does not exist, I was allowed to be fairly liberal in deciding what the story behind Hope & Anchor is and what their goals as a business are. I’ll explain the store’s background here:

“Hope & Anchor Hardware is an institution which serves a diverse range of customers. Whether you’re a major contractor looking for some extra hurricane straps or a 10 year old looking for a few more lengths of slot-car track, Hope & Anchor has you covered.”

Something like that. Diversity of customers is the theme.

Solution

To highlight the diversity of clientele served, Hope & Anchor's' identity is built around a logo that can evolve into different types of homes and businesses to represent the various regional, economic, and occupational backgrounds of shoppers. A warehouse, apartment building, and suburban home are included among others.

Also included is a business card that doubles as sandpaper. Cause why not?
