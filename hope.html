Challenge

One of my assignments in Color Studio at RISD was to develop branding for the fictional Rhode Island hardware store, Hope & Anchor Hardware. We were to concoct a brief story or theme behind the store’s identity and then develop a brand identity displayed in a full-fledged style guide.

While “hope” and “anchor” do have historical significance in Rhode Island, I chose to develop an identity that pulled more directly from personal experiences in hardware stores. One particularly memorable moment came when I was 6 or 7 and I discovered my local hardware store’s basement filled model airplanes, thinking that someone had put aside a section of the store just for me. The models transformed my idea of what a hardware store was: not just a place for my Dad to buy drill bits but a place that looked out for little kids like me too. When working on the branding project, I reflected further, marvelling that a hardware store could be, in one instance, completely enchanting a 6 year old, providing a contactractor with just the right washer, and selling my Mom christmas lights.

A hardware store serves customers with a wide range of backgrounds and needs, yet most would probably still describe a traditional customer as someone similar to my dad: a middle aged man looking for drill bits. I chose to develop a brand identity that responds to this mischaracterization—an identity that expands the idea of who a traditional hardware store customer is.

Solution

To highlight the diversity of clientele, Hope & Anchor’s identity is built around a logo that can evolve into different types of homes and businesses. The varying shapes represent the various regional, economic, and occupational backgrounds of shoppers. A warehouse, apartment building, and suburban home are included among others.

For the accompanying type, I decided that a less muscular typeface would make “less muscular” clientele feel more at home. The pairing of all caps with a more delicate weight aim to strike a balance between the traditional products a hardware store is associated with and the less traditional.

Also included is a business card with 150 grit sandpaper backing. Because why not?
